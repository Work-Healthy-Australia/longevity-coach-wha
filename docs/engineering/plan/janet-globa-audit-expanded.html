Strong Cross-Market Position — Regional Plays Required

Janet's combination of biological age scoring, supplement protocols, B2B billing, and gamification places it ahead of most coaching platforms globally. The global landscape is regionally fragmented: the US market is dominated by clinical depth (Geviti, HolistiCare); the ANZ market has one dominant longevity player (Vively) — consumer-only, no B2B; and the APAC market is ruled by insurance-linked wellness (AIA Vitality, Prudential Pulse) and B2B corporate coaching (Naluri, ThoughtFull) — neither with longevity science. Janet has a real lane in all three, but each requires different go-to-market and feature priorities.

Where Janet Leads Globally

Combination of bio age + multi-domain risk scoring in one platform matched only by clinical platforms charging $500+/month. Janet delivers this in a consumer + coach hybrid. No global competitor combines all three layers.

💊
AI Supplement Protocol Generation

No mainstream coaching platform globally generates personalised supplement protocols from health data. Geviti does it within a closed ecosystem. Janet's is open and affiliatable — a monetisation moat in every region.

🏢
B2B Longevity — Empty Quadrant Globally

In ANZ, Vively is consumer-only and MFM/Biogenix are clinic-only. In APAC, Naluri/ThoughtFull have B2B but no longevity science. Janet sits in the empty B2B-longevity quadrant in every market — globally defensible if built to regional compliance.

🎮
Gamification — Unique in Longevity, Proven in APAC

No longevity competitor has gamification. AIA Vitality's points model proves engagement value at scale across Asia. Janet is the only platform combining gamification with serious longevity science — the engagement depth AIA's wellness arm wants but can't build internally.

🤝
No-Staff Ecosystem Partner Model

Partnership architecture (coaches, labs, GPs, supplement vendors, meal providers) has no direct global analogue. In ANZ this maps to HCANZA-accredited coaches; in SEA to insurance partners and corporate health vendors. The model scales margin without headcount in every market.

Priority Gap Analysis

What Must Be Built to Win Globally

🔴 Critical — Build Now
Lab Result Import & Biomarker Parsing

Every clinical longevity competitor (Vively AU, Chi Longevity SG, InsideTracker, Geviti) offers PDF lab upload with automatic biomarker extraction. Without this, bio age scores rely on user-entered estimates rather than real pathology data.

→ Vively imports panels from 2,000+ AU collection centres. Table-stakes globally for the longevity category.
🔴 Critical — Build Now
Live Wearable Data Normalisation

Terra API is partially implemented but data isn't yet flowing into the bio age engine in real time. HRV, sleep, RHR and recovery are inputs that Vively, Naluri, AIA Vitality, and 5 of 6 US competitors already use live.

→ Without live wearable data, Janet's bio age engine is a static questionnaire. Competitors update risk scores daily from Oura, Whoop, Apple Watch.
🔵 Regional — APAC Critical
Insurer Partnership API (Vitality-Style)

In APAC, AIA Vitality and Prudential Pulse own consumer distribution at insurer-subsidised pricing (S$8/month). Without an insurer integration layer, Janet cannot enter the APAC consumer market at competitive pricing.

→ Pilot with one regional insurer (Income, AIA, FWD, Prudential) before any APAC consumer launch. Consumer-direct pricing without insurer subsidy won't compete below AIA's S$8/mo benchmark.
🔵 Regional — APAC Critical
Multi-Language + Cultural Localisation

Naluri runs in EN/MS/ID/TH. ThoughtFull supports 28+ languages. Beyond translation, supplement protocols need halal certification awareness, TCM integration, and Asian dietary patterns (rice-based, hawker, Indonesian street food).

→ Minimum viable SEA launch: EN, Bahasa Malaysia/Indonesia, Mandarin, Thai. AI translation alone is not sufficient — protocol content needs review by regional dietitians.
🔵 Regional — ANZ Critical
HCANZA Coach Partnership + TGA Compliance

In Australia, credible coaches run through HCANZA (Health Coaches Australia and New Zealand Association). TGA tightly regulates supplement and health claims — Janet's protocol content must be TGA-compliant before any AU consumer launch.

→ Become an HCANZA-recognised platform. TGA compliance review of all supplement content. Without these, no Australian practitioner will recommend Janet.
🔵 Regional — All Markets
Multi-Region Data Compliance

HIPAA covers the US. APAC requires Singapore PDPA, Indonesian PDP Law, Thai PDPA. ANZ requires Privacy Act 1988 + Australian Privacy Principles. Each region requires specific data residency, breach notification timelines, and consent frameworks.

→ ISO 27001 helps but does not substitute for local law compliance. Engage local privacy counsel per launch market before handling live user data.
🟡 High — Next Sprint
Epigenetic / DNA Clock Integration

InsideTracker, TruDiagnostic (US) and Chi Longevity, MFM, Biogenix (APAC/AU) all offer or integrate epigenetic biological age. DunedinPACE and GrimAge are the new standard. Partnership model — no build required.

→ TruDiagnostic globally, MFM or Biogenix in AU, Chi Longevity in SG. Import epigenetic score into bio age dashboard via partnership agreement.
🟡 High — Next Sprint
Supplement Affiliate Commission Tracking (Live)

In AU, supplement partners must be TGA-listed. In SEA, halal-certified supply chains are critical (Indonesia MUI certification). Each region requires a separate supplier integration. Coach incentive model depends on commission accuracy.

→ AU: TGA-listed partners (Activated Probiotics, BioCeuticals). SEA: halal-certified vendors per market. Every week delayed costs coach activation.
🟣 Medium — Q3 Build
Microbiome / Gut Pipeline

Singapore's AMILI is launching a personalised nutrition app with Google in April 2026. Viome leads globally. Both are partnership opportunities for data import into Janet's supplement protocol engine.

→ AMILI partnership is strategic for Singapore — local data, local trust, local regulatory standing. Viome for US and global reach.
🟣 Medium — Q3 Build
White-Label / Clinic Licensing Tier

HolistiCare's model is white-label clinic licensing (US). In AU, this maps to functional medicine clinics; in SEA, to private hospital networks (Bumrungrad, Mount Elizabeth, Gleneagles, Siloam). Each targets high-net-worth international patients.

→ APAC private hospital groups are actively seeking longevity programs. White-label is the entry path that avoids competing with insurer distribution.
